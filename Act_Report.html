Act_Report: WeRateDogs Twitter¶

by Kathy Mirzaei

WeRateDogs Dataset¶

The "We Rate Dogs" is a Twitter account that rates people's dogs with a humorous comment about the dog. This Twitter archive contains over 5000 tweets, which have been filtered to create the enhanced archive that forms the basis of this analysis. The time frame for the posts are from November 15, 2015 to August 1, 2017.

The retweet count and favourite count for each tweet were not included in the enhanced archived at first, this data was downloaded using the twitter developer API by using the tweet id from the archive file.

In addition, the image prediction dataset was used. This data was available from Udacity and contained the top 3 predictions for a dog breed based on their image.

Wrangling data¶

The data was assessed from all the three different sources, both visually and programmatically for quality and tidiness issues. The quality of the data was assessed based on the four dimentions of completeness, validity, accuracy, and consistency. The tidiness of the data was assessed based on the structure of the data. At the start point the archive had 2356 tweets, and at the final part the clean data had about 1941 clean, combined tweets.

Pelple LOVE the Retrivers¶

In the image prediction data, when the image from the tweets was fed through the neural network, we got 85% prediction, and we identified 113 different dog breeds. The confidence of the predictions had a range as well, and many of the tweets were low quality images. Despite this, only 300 of the tweets did not really identified as a dog. Top 5 dog breeds with the most number of tweets are the same as the highest total favorite counts (green bar plot above). Looking at the plots, there are 8 breeds that are in common: golden_retriever, Labrador_retriever, Pembroke, Chihuahua, pug,chow,Samoyed,and malamute.

Strong relationship between Favourite Count and Retweet Count:¶

In the below plot you will see a strong relationship between retweet counts and favourite counts. The higher the favourite count is, the more re-tweet it gets. The plot below demonstrates a strong positive correlation between the two variables.

The colors added to each tweet denotes the rating blue is lower, and purple is higher. It is noticable that there are many more purple than blues which shos the facourite counts and retweet counts are higher in number.
